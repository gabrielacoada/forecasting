## Questions to Ask 

### 1. Satellite Model Validation 

> "We found that single-equation satellite models — the same approach Fed DFAST, ECB, and Bank of England use for stress testing — outperform VARs for scenario-conditional forecasting in our data. The satellite beats the AR baseline by 23% for C&I (DM p=0.015) and 19% for consumer (DM p=0.077). We plan to present the satellite as our primary forecasting model and use the VAR for causal narrative (impulse responses, Granger causality). Does this two-model structure make sense to you?"

**Why ask**: This is our single biggest methodological choice. Getting buy-in now avoids a presentation surprise.

### 2. Consumer Drivers — Reporting a Negative Result 

> "You asked us to look beyond unemployment and interest rates for consumer drivers. We tested Case-Shiller house prices, real disposable income, and Michigan consumer sentiment. None improved the model — BIC still prefers just unemployment + Fed Funds. The NGFS NiGEM data also has house price and income scenario paths, but adding them doesn't help OOS. Should we present this as 'we tested it and here's why rates dominate,' or do you think there are other drivers we're missing?"

**Why ask**: They specifically pushed on this in Q&A 1. Showing we tested and got a null result is strong — but get their reaction.

### 3. Confidence Bands — What Do They Actually Want? 

> "You mentioned wanting confidence bands. We can produce: (a) parametric forecast intervals from the model (plus/minus 1.96 sigma), (b) cross-IAM-family ranges showing how GCAM vs REMIND vs MESSAGEix diverge, or (c) both as a combined fan chart. Which is more useful from a decision-making perspective? The IAM spread is often larger than the statistical uncertainty."

**Why ask**: They explicitly said "a lot of value from the confidence bands." Clarifying what kind avoids wasted work.

### 4. Actionable Insights — Concrete Examples [MEDIUM]

> "You said 'don't just say loans drop 2% — dig into it.' Can you give us an example of the kind of executive-level takeaway you find most compelling? For instance, is it more like 'delayed action on climate policy costs X% in additional loan losses' or 'C&I portfolios are more climate-sensitive than consumer — here's why that matters for allocation'?"

**Why ask**: They were vague last time. Getting a concrete example helps frame the whole presentation narrative.

### 5. Leading/Lagging Confirmation [MEDIUM]

> "You warned about accidentally using future data. Our satellite model uses lagged regressors (t-1) for everything — so last quarter's unemployment predicts this quarter's loan growth. Is there anything else you'd flag about timing alignment, especially with the NGFS annual scenario data?"

**Why ask**: Quick confirmation that our approach is sound. Also shows we took their warning seriously.

### 6. Presentation Logistics [LOW but practical]

> "A few logistics for April 9: How technical should the 30 minutes be? Are we presenting to just your team of 4, or will others attend? Any preference on structure — e.g., do you want us to walk through the model step by step, or focus more on scenario results and strategic implications?"

**Why ask**: Knowing the audience determines the pitch level.
